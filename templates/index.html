🌈 Searles Elementary — science fair · let’s explore the air!

🔭 The big question

Which room has the nicest-feeling air on our sensor — and does it change from morning to lunch to evening?

We use a tiny air chip (BME680) and a Raspberry Pi to turn invisible air stuff into a 0–100 game score on this page. It is our experiment’s rules, not a government website — but it is real hardware! Move the box, pick the room down below, and the clock says if it is morning, lunch, or evening.

The answers are short — you can’t break anything by looking.

What is the 0 to 100 number?

It is a line we draw in software from the sensor’s gas reading (bigger “gas” ohms in our model usually land higher on the 0–100 line). Bigger = “happier on our scale,” not a doctor’s test.

Do the colored lights on the box match the website?

Yes! Green, yellow, and red on the build line up with the good / okay / yikes ideas we coded — same story in two places.

What should I try first?

Change the room when the Pi moves, then scroll to Air score and watch the wiggly line. Tap the four number tiles to see what each one is!

For teachers / parents — one technical sentence

Score: linear map of BME680 gas resistance per air_quality in config.yaml; labels use good_min / moderate_min; room changes gate logs so rows match the space.

🎮 Three ways to play with it

Room picker Every time the box moves, choose the new room so the graph tells the truth.

Breath & mist A tiny whoosh of breath (gentle!) can make humidity jump on purpose.

Number tiles Under the live air score, tap each square — surprise facts pop out.
