Where in our home is the air cleanest—and how does that change from morning, to lunch, to evening?

Sophia Merce G. Manaol

That is what this experiment tests. The air gets a score from 0 to 100—bigger number = cleaner air. When you move the small computer to a new room, pick that room on the list below. The clock on the project tells morning, lunch, and evening. Colored lights on the box match the key here:

Green — nice & clear Yellow — okay, keep an eye on it Red — time for a window or fan if you can

Try this with your class!

  • Change the room when the box moves—watch the line chart start fresh.
  • Ask a friend to breathe gently near the sensor and peek at humidity.
  • Tap the colored number boxes below for a quick “what is this?” hint.
