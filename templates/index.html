🔭 The big question

Which room has the nicest-feeling air in our class test — and does it change from morning to lunch to evening?

We built a tiny “air brain” inside the box and a little computer to turn invisible air stuff into a 0–100 game score on this page. The labels and math are our project’s — and the gadget is real! Move the box, pick the room down below, and the clock says if it is morning, lunch, or evening.

🌬️ Air trivia — 3 fun facts (tap to open; optional for your report!)

Air trivia and fun facts

Open any card for a short read.

One breath is packed with water you can’t see

Warm exhaled air holds lots of water vapor. Breathe near the box (gently) and the humidity number can jump — same idea as a foggy bathroom mirror after a hot shower, just measured by our air box.

What is the “air sniff” (gas) number?

Inside the box is a teeny hot speck that “asks” the air a chemistry question. We show the answer as a big air-sniff number on the page. A bigger number here often means the air looks fresher in our project’s game after the little heater has settled — it is not a super-nose, it is a science trick in code!

Why should you pick the right room in the list?

A kitchen, a bedroom, and a yard are not the same: different warmth, different moisture, and different “bits” in the air. Telling the page where the box is helps the graph tell the truth when you compare morning, lunch, and evening in each space.
